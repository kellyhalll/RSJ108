5 Tips For A Sports Journalist

Assembly time: 1 Hour
Effectiveness rating: 100% effective

If you’ve ever dreamed of turning your love for sports into a career on the sidelines or behind the headlines, you’re in the right place. Sports journalism is fast-paced, competitive, and exciting—but getting started takes the right tools and mindset. This website breaks down five key tips that will help you build skills, gain experience, and make your mark in the industry.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Determination
  • Good Mindset
  • A Love For Sports
  • Strong Writing Abilties
  • Social Skills
  • Hardworking Skills

  1. Honing Your Writing Skills.

    Start a blog. One way you can hone your writing skills is by starting your own blog. First, decide what your blog will focus on. Since your interest is in sports journalism, start a sports blog detailing commentary, highlights, and results from sporting events you attend. Also, fill your blog with stories and interviews you have conducted with athletes and coaches.

  2. Obtaining Training In Journalism.

    Obtain a journalism degree. Obtaining a degree in journalism will ensure that you will have the basic necessary training in order to become a sports journalist. Research journalism and communication programs that meet your personal interests. You can begin by requesting information from journalism schools that interest you. You can also meet with the program's representatives in order to see if it is right for you. Tell the representatives your future goals in order for them to assess if the program meets your needs.

  3. Specializing in Sports Journalism.

    Become a sports expert. In order to write about sports, you need to be an expert in all things related to sports. There are several things you can do in order to develop a broad knowledge base. First, watch and attend as many sports games as you can. Practice memorizing coach and player names and positions on the team. Also, watch and take notes on interviews with players and coaches in order to learn the kinds of questions journalists ask.

  4. Landing A Sports Journalist Job.

    Create a work portfolio. Once you have published several well-written pieces, begin compiling your best pieces for your portfolio. You do not need to include all your writing pieces. A few solid articles are better than a lot of mediocre pieces. Your writing pieces should represent you and your writing interests. If you have several pieces that cover different sports, categorize them as such. For example, have a separate category for football, tennis, and soccer.

  5. Coming All Together.

    Network and maintain relationships. Remember to network during your undergraduate studies, internship, and freelancing experience. Create relationships with your supervisor and other writers. These relationships can lead to job opportunities in the future. You can create relationships with your supervisor by being proactive and by creating quality-writing pieces..
